Project Overview

This project proposes the development of a comprehensive, AI-driven mobile platform aimed at enhancing road safety and disaster preparedness in Sri Lanka. The system integrates four key components: Driver Behavior Monitoring, Accident Detection, Traffic Sign Recognition, and Real-Time Flood Prediction and Navigation.

Utilizing smartphone sensors, computer vision, machine learning, and real-time data, the platform will deliver alerts and guidance to drivers to reduce the risk and impact of road accidents and natural disasters, particularly floods. The solution is designed to be accessible, affordable, and scalable for widespread adoption.

üîß In-Scope Components

Driver Behavior Monitoring Module

  • Utilizes the smartphone's front camera to monitor facial expressions, eye movement, and head orientation
  • Detects signs of drowsiness, distraction, and aggressive driving using AI and computer vision
  • Provides real-time alerts and personalized feedback through an in-app interface
  • Includes voice-based interaction via Natural Language Processing (NLP) and gamified engagement features

Accident Detection Module

  • Leverages smartphone accelerometers and gyroscopes to detect sudden deceleration and impact
  • Sends automated emergency alerts with GPS location and incident details to nearby authorities and emergency contacts
  • Functions without external hardware, making it cost-effective and scalable
  • Includes crash severity assessment and automatic emergency service contact

Traffic Sign Recognition Module

  • Uses mobile cameras and deep learning (e.g., YOLO or CNN models) to recognize road signs in real-time
  • Provides instant alerts to drivers for compliance and safety
  • Supports detection of common Sri Lankan road signs in Sinhala, Tamil, and English
  • Includes speed limit monitoring and violation warnings

Flood Prediction and Navigation Module

  • Combines historical flood records, real-time weather updates, and machine learning to assess flood risk
  • Offers dynamic route suggestions using Google Maps API based on flood conditions
  • Focuses on flood-prone areas such as Jaffna, Kalutara, Rathnapura, Gampaha, and Ampara
  • Provides early warning system for extreme weather conditions

The development of a Smart Driver Support and Disaster Alert System builds upon significant advancements in several areas of artificial intelligence, mobile computing, and environmental monitoring. This section reviews key prior works related to the four main components of the system.

Driver Behavior Monitoring

Driver fatigue and distraction have been widely studied due to their major contribution to road accidents. A system proposed by Soujanya et al. [1] used convolutional neural networks (CNNs) to analyze eye movements and facial expressions to detect drowsiness with high accuracy. Abouelnaga et al. [2] developed a deep learning-based framework that classifies distracted behaviors like texting, eating, or talking using real-world driving footage.

Key Innovation: Our system extends these concepts by utilizing smartphone-based video input and lightweight AI models, making it more accessible for users in developing regions like Sri Lanka.
üö®

Accident Detection

In the area of mobile-based accident detection, Kaur et al. [3] proposed a smartphone-based solution that detects crashes using accelerometer and gyroscope data. The system applies threshold-based detection to trigger emergency alerts, providing a practical and low-cost solution. Similarly, commercial platforms like GM's OnStar use vehicle telematics to detect collisions [4], but these are limited to specific car models.

Key Innovation: Our solution builds on open-source methods and integrates GPS-based location sharing and SMS alerts for broader accessibility.
üö¶

Traffic Sign Recognition

Traffic sign recognition has advanced with the introduction of deep learning. Cire»ôan et al. [5] achieved over 99% accuracy using a multi-column deep neural network (MCDNN) trained on the GTSRB dataset. Real-time recognition methods such as YOLO (You Only Look Once) and SSD (Single Shot Multibox Detector) have made it feasible to deploy object detection on mobile devices [6], [7].

Key Innovation: Our system uses such models, trained on Sri Lankan traffic sign datasets, to offer accurate detection with real-time voice feedback to the driver.

Flood Forecasting Systems

Flood forecasting systems have improved significantly with the adoption of machine learning. Jain et al. [8] used support vector machines and decision tree models to forecast flood-prone regions. More recently, deep learning models like LSTM have been used for time-series flood prediction [9]. Integration of weather data and routing platforms, as done in [10], provides real-time navigation to avoid flood-affected areas.

Key Innovation: Our system leverages similar techniques with a focus on local flood history and rainfall data, using APIs and Google Maps integration to guide drivers safely through flood-prone regions.

Accident Detection Limitations

High Priority

While smartphone-based accident detection has been explored, many systems rely solely on static threshold models for accelerometer data interpretation. These models can misclassify sudden braking or a dropped phone as a collision, resulting in false alarms.

Key Issues:
  • Lack of contextual analysis (road conditions, speed patterns, driving history)
  • Limited automated emergency alerting with location tracking
  • Poor performance in low-connectivity areas
  • Inadequate integration with local emergency services
üö¶

Traffic Sign Recognition Gaps

Medium Priority

The majority of traffic sign recognition (TSR) research has focused on standardized datasets like GTSRB and systems designed for European or North American traffic environments. These models often fail to recognize non-standard or region-specific signs.

Key Issues:
  • Lack of localized datasets for South Asian conditions
  • Poor recognition of Sinhala and Tamil text on signs
  • Limited real-time audio feedback systems
  • Inadequate handling of weathered or occluded signs
üåä

Flood Prediction System Shortcomings

Low Priority

Flood forecasting systems currently in use either rely on satellite imagery, which lacks real-time accuracy, or simulation models that are computationally expensive and not mobile-friendly.

Key Issues:
  • Lack of lightweight, mobile-first flood prediction engines
  • Limited real-time flood-aware routing capabilities
  • Poor integration of historical flood data with live weather updates
  • Inadequate coverage of regional flood patterns
